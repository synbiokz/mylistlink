Here’s the “why these are so sticky” take, then a practical teardown you can lift for your own build.
Big picture: the shared playbook
All five sites succeed by combining three reinforcing loops:
1. Entity hubs → infinite rabbit holes. Every person/book/film/series has a canonical “hub” page that fans orbit around. Those hubs interlink (cast ↔ titles, authors ↔ books, studios ↔ catalogs), forming a navigable knowledge graph that always gives you a next click. (IMDb calls out personalization on top of this, based on your ratings & watchlist. (IMDb Help))

2. Low-friction micro-contributions → compounding UGC. One-click actions (rate ★, upvote ⬆, “add to list/shelf,” “mark watched/reading”) let casual users contribute signal without “writing a review.” That steady trickle powers rankings, recommendations, karma, and reputation systems (IMDb’s weighted ratings, Reddit karma). (IMDb Help, IMDb, Reddit Help)

3. Status, rituals, and authority. They motivate returns via:

   * Status (Reddit karma; IMDb badges/Top 250; MAL list stats). (Reddit Help, Wikipedia)

   * Rituals (Goodreads annual Reading Challenge, MAL seasonal charts). (Wikipedia)

   * Authority layers (Rotten Tomatoes’ Tomatometer & Verified Audience programs). (Rotten Tomatoes, Rotten Tomatoes Editorial)

These loops also create SEO moats: millions of fresh, interlinked pages with structured signals (ratings, reviews, lists), which search engines love.

Breakdowns by dimension
Design
Common threads
      * Entity-first layout: hero metadata up top (poster/cover, title, key stats), then tabs/sections (reviews, lists, trivia, cast/characters).

      * Prominent micro-actions: rate, add to shelf/list/watchlist, upvote/comment—always near the fold.

      * “What’s next” rails: related titles/authors, staff picks, “people also liked,” seasonal or trending modules.

Site notes
         * IMDb: Design funnels you into watchlist/ratings and cross-links people ↔ titles. Personalized “Top Picks” lives near the surface. (IMDb Help)

         * Goodreads: Dense but utilitarian; shelves & quotes are first-class; the yearly “My Year in Books” recap is a sticky ritual. (Wikipedia)

         * Reddit: Card/list views emphasize quick scanning; voting affordances are the primary visual anchor. (Reddit Inc)

         * Rotten Tomatoes: Dual authority signals (Tomatometer + Audience/Verified Audience) are visually prominent; score-detail explainer links are nearby. (Rotten Tomatoes)

         * MyAnimeList (MAL): Power-user customization (profile/list CSS), plus seasonal/airing views that spark frequent returns. (Wikipedia)

Levels (information architecture)
Typical hierarchy (reuse this):
            * /entity (title/book/anime/person/author) → /reviews, /ratings, /lists/shelves, /news/trivia, /related

            * /user (profile) → /ratings, /lists/shelves, /comments

            * /discover (charts, seasonal, genre, popularity)

            * /community (subs/forums/clubs), /pro (IMDbPro), /help

Distinctives
               * IMDb: Help center exposes Lists/Watchlist/Check-ins as core “track → recommend” primitives. (IMDb Help)

               * Goodreads: “Shelves” (default + custom) structure the whole experience; groups/book clubs live under community. (Wikipedia)

               * Reddit: Topic-first (communities/subreddits) → threads → comments; reputation (karma) per user spans communities. (Reddit Help)

               * Rotten Tomatoes: Each title bundles Critics consensus + Audience score, with score-detail & eligibility criteria. (Rotten Tomatoes)

               * MAL: Entity hubs plus user lists, reviews, clubs; ranking formulas and seasonal indexes are surfaced. (Wikipedia)

User interaction (viewing vs. contributing)
                  * IMDb: View + light contribute (rate, review, lists). Ratings are weighted to limit manipulation. Pro users can claim/manage pages via IMDbPro. (IMDb Help)

                  * Goodreads: Heavy social contribution—ratings, reviews, comments, groups, author Q&As; annual Challenges drive repeat posting. (Wikipedia)

                  * Reddit: Creation & moderation are central; up/downvotes + karma define visibility and identity. (Reddit Help)

                  * Rotten Tomatoes: Critics supply the Tomatometer; audiences rate; recent emphasis on Verified audience programs to boost trust. (Rotten Tomatoes, GLAAD)

                  * MAL: Track & score lists (1–10), write reviews/recs, join clubs, style your lists—collection + community. (Wikipedia)

Performance
Shared playbook
                     * Precomputed aggregates (scores, leaderboards) and heavy caching/CDNs so entity pages load fast despite massive traffic.

                     * Progressive hydration (content first, widgets later) and deferred loading for long comment/review threads.

Concrete signals you can adopt
                        * Compute & cache title/author aggregates offline; treat score widgets as edge-cached fragments.

                        * Rate limits + anti-brigading (IMDb’s weighted ratings; RT’s verification) to keep signals clean under load. (IMDb Help, Rotten Tomatoes)

SEO & growth
                           * Entity saturation: One canonical page per title/book/person with rich interlinking → long-tail capture.

                           * Freshness via UGC: New ratings/reviews/comments keep pages “alive.”

                           * Structured signals: Clear rating models and consensus blurbs (e.g., Tomatometer) become compelling SERP snippets. (Rotten Tomatoes)

                           * Cross-ecosystem hooks: Goodreads ↔ Kindle/Audible; RT ↔ Fandango; Reddit’s deep linkability of threads. (Wikipedia)

Programming under the hood (what’s public vs. what to emulate)
                              * Reddit: First-party API is GraphQL; migrated heavy paths from Python to Go for lower latency and better concurrency. Use a gateway + federated subgraphs pattern. (This is straight from Reddit Engineering.) (Reddit)

                              * Goodreads: Amazon job listings reference Java and Ruby on Rails in production support—expect classic web MVC + services under Amazon infra. (Don’t overfit; stacks evolve.) (amazon.jobs)

                              * IMDb: Publicly details algorithms, not stack: weighted ratings, recommendations from collaborative signals. Also ships IMDbPro & exposes datasets for external analysis. Emulate: data pipelines + abuse-resistant scoring. (IMDb Help, Amazon Web Services, Inc.)

                              * Rotten Tomatoes: Product logic is documented (Tomatometer/Audience/Verified programs); architecturally you’d mirror it as ingestion pipelines + curator review tooling + public aggregates. (Rotten Tomatoes)

                              * MAL: Feature set suggests classic LAMP/Rails-style app with forums, lists, ranking math, and user theming. Ownership recently changed to Gaudiy, which signals future platform investments; plan for API boundaries and profile theming safely. (Wikipedia)

Domain names (brand & recall)
                                 * IMDb.com – short acronym; authoritative/industry-centric vibe.

                                 * goodreads.com – literal promise/benefit (“good reads”).

                                 * reddit.com – phonetic pun (“read it”); casual tone matches community ethos.

                                 * rottentomatoes.com – vivid metaphor → memorable; fits a binary “fresh/rotten” system.

                                 * myanimelist.net – functional & ownership (“my”), aligns with personal collection tracking; .net acceptable given early origin and niche authority.

Other facets that matter
                                    * Trust & integrity systems:

                                       * Weighted or verified ratings to dampen brigading/trolls (IMDb weighting; RT verification & eligibility thresholds). (IMDb Help, Rotten Tomatoes)

                                       * Clear community rules & mod tools at scale (Reddit). (Reddit Inc)

                                          * Pro layers & creator tools: IMDbPro for professionals; Goodreads Author Program; both create supply-side loyalty. (IMDb Help, Goodreads)

                                          * Seasonality & events: MAL seasonal charts; Goodreads yearly recaps; RT awards/season packages—all drive predictable spikes. (Wikipedia)

                                          * Ownership synergies:

                                             * IMDb & Goodreads under Amazon enable distribution hooks (Kindle/Audible, Prime/Freevee tie-ins). (Wikipedia)

                                             * Rotten Tomatoes under Fandango aligns with ticketing & verified receipts. (Wikipedia)

                                             * MAL now under Gaudiy hints at fan-data & community tech pushes. (Wikipedia)

What to implement for your “fantastic website”
                                                               1. Design the entity graph first. Define your canonical objects, their bidirectional links, and the minimal above-the-fold facts + micro-actions.

                                                               2. Ship micro-contribution early. One-click saves/follows/ratings before longform reviews.

                                                               3. Add one ritual + one status signal. E.g., an annual challenge and a visible, game-resistant reputation metric.

                                                               4. Protect the signals. Start with IMDb-style weighted scores; add verification where feasible (purchase, domain, tenure). (IMDb Help, Rotten Tomatoes)

                                                               5. Scale with a gateway. If you’ll have multiple clients (web/app), a GraphQL gateway + federated services is a proven pattern for iteration at scale. (Reddit’s journey is a clean reference.) (Reddit)

                                                               6. Lean into SEO via entities + internal links. Ensure every entity page is richly interlinked and frequently refreshed by UGC.

                                                               7. Create a “pro/creator” surface to deepen supply-side engagement (claim pages, add media, run giveaways). (IMDb Help, Goodreads)
