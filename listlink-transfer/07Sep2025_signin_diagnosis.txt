Here are the **rock-solid, widely used** signup/sign-in options that are free (or have generous free tiers), well-documented, and composable. I grouped them by approach and then tell you which I’d pick for ListLink (Next.js + Prisma).

# Shortlist for your stack (Next.js + Prisma)

* **Auth.js (formerly NextAuth)** — free, open-source, built for Next.js, tons of OAuth providers, plus credentials/magic-link options. My #1 for ListLink if you want to own sessions in your app. ([authjs.dev][1], [next-auth.js.org][2])
* **Supabase Auth** — great if you’re moving to Postgres anyway; Auth integrates tightly with RLS policies and has a clearly documented, generous **free** tier. ([Supabase][3])

# The usual suspects (all proven, composable)

## 1) Libraries you run inside your app

* **Auth.js (NextAuth)** – Next.js-first auth with >80 pre-made OAuth providers, plus Credentials and Email/Magic Link. Free & open source. ([authjs.dev][1], [next-auth.js.org][2])
* **Passport.js** – battle-tested Node/Express middleware with hundreds of strategies (username/password, Google, etc.). Very flexible if you want to wire low-level flows yourself. ([Passport.js][4])

## 2) Backend platforms with built-in Auth

* **Supabase Auth** – JWT-based auth integrated with Postgres + Row Level Security; supports password, magic links/OTP, and social/SSO. Generous **Free** plan (MAU limits published on pricing page). ([Supabase][3])
* **Firebase Authentication** – easy SDKs and optional drop-in UI; supports email/password, phone, and major OAuth providers; strong docs and “no-cost to start” pricing. ([Firebase][5])
* **Appwrite Auth** – open-source BaaS; supports email/password, OAuth, anonymous, magic links, etc., with good docs. ([appwrite.io][6])

## 3) Managed identity providers (hosted, prebuilt UI)

* **Clerk** – polished prebuilt components for web/mobile; generous **Free** tier (first 10k MAUs) and solid docs. Great if you want to move fast with minimal custom code. ([Clerk][7])
* **Auth0** – enterprise-grade identity with a **Free** developer plan; broad protocol support and excellent documentation/examples. ([Auth0][8])
* **AWS Cognito** – widely used on AWS; “user pools” provide OIDC/OAuth and social login; good fit if you’re already on AWS. ([AWS Documentation][9])

## 4) Self-hosted identity servers (maximum control)

* **Keycloak** – open-source IAM server (OIDC/OAuth2/SAML), social login, theming, admin console; very mature. Best if you need SSO, multi-realm, or on-prem control. ([Keycloak][10])
* **FusionAuth (Community)** – free to self-host; modern features (including WebAuthn/passkeys) with clear docs; paid cloud available later if needed. ([FusionAuth][11])
* **Ory Kratos** – open-source, headless identity service for self-service flows (login, registration, recovery, verification); cloud or self-host. ([Ory Corp][12])

# Composability cheat-sheet

All of the above support **multiple “ways to log in”** (email/password, OAuth social logins, magic links/OTP). Notable add-ons:

* **Passkeys/WebAuthn**: directly supported (production-ready) in FusionAuth; experimental in Auth.js today. ([FusionAuth][13], [authjs.dev][14])
* **SSO/SAML/OIDC**: turnkey in Auth0, Cognito, Keycloak, FusionAuth; available in Supabase via external IdPs/SSO features. ([Auth0][8], [AWS Documentation][9], [Keycloak][10])

# What I’d pick for ListLink right now

* **If we stay with Prisma and our own DB**: **Auth.js** (Next.js-native, fast to wire, zero SaaS lock-in). ([authjs.dev][1])
* **If we’re moving the DB to Supabase**: **Supabase Auth** (tight RLS integration; one provider for DB + auth + storage; solid free tier). ([Supabase][3])
